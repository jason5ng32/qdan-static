我最喜欢的 TED 演讲

我看了一些 TED 演讲，下面的清单中列出了我最喜欢的几个。

  1. How schools kill creativity (by Ken Robinson)

    这是 TED 非常有名的一个演讲。当 Robinson 在演讲中说到 「My contention is that creativity now is as important in education as literacy 」的时候，我被深深地触动了一下。

    到底有多少教育会真正做到鼓励「创造力」，而不是试图抑制它？

    How schools kill creativity
    Sir Ken Robinson makes an entertaining and profoundly moving case for creating an education system that nurtures (rather than undermines) creativity.
      ted.com

    • Why 30 is not the new 20 (by Meg Jay)

      这算是我看过的启蒙 TED ，中文被翻成「20岁，不可挥霍的光阴」，Meg Jay 在演讲中提了三点她认为可以让 20 岁增值的事情：

      1. 积累身份资本：进行一些增加自我价值的事情；

      2. 利用弱连接：那些「圈子外」的人会给你带来新机会；

      3. 选择家庭：从现在开始积极选择你想要的人和生活。

      Why 30 is not the new 20
      Clinical psychologist Meg Jay has a bold message for twentysomethings: Contrary to popular belief, your 20s are not a throwaway decade. In this provocative talk, Jay says that just because marriage, work and kids are happening later in life, doesn’t mean you can’t start planning now. She gives 3 pieces of advice for how twentysomethings can re-claim adulthood in the defining decade of their lives.
        ted.com

      • A kinder, gentler philosophy of success (by Alain de Botton)

        Alain de Botton 是我非常喜欢的作家，演讲有关「温和的成功哲学」。阿兰说，嫉妒带给我们强烈的焦虑感，不过我们想要的其实是物质背后的情感反馈，这很值得深思。

        在演讲的后半部分阿兰用坚定而强烈的语气说到，一个人不可能在所有事情上成功。「We hear a lot of talk about work-life balance. Nonsense. You can't have it all. You can't. 」

        TED 上与 success 有关的演讲非常多，这是我最喜欢的一个，或许每个人都应该思索什么才是自己心目中定义的成功。

        A kinder, gentler philosophy of success
        Alain de Botton examines our ideas of success and failure -- and questions the assumptions underlying these two judgments. Is success always earned? Is failure? He makes an eloquent, witty case to move beyond snobbery to find true pleasure in our work.
          ted.com

        • Gaming can make a better world (by Jane McGonigal)

          这也是非常有名的一个演讲，另外 Jane 写过一本叫做《游戏改变生活》的书同样十分畅销。它颠覆了大多数人惯有的认知：原来游戏并不总是一件坏事，我们也可以借助游戏的力量，让生活变得更精彩。

          日常生活中的许多事情都可以用「游戏思维」解决。虽然不可能次次奏效，但是给尝试自己设置一些小的激励和奖励措施，总可以得到一点点改变吧？

          Gaming can make a better world
          Games like World of Warcraft give players the means to save worlds, and incentive to learn the habits of heroes. What if we could harness this gamer power to solve real-world problems? Jane McGonigal says we can, and explains how.
            ted.com

          • The power of introverts (by Susan Cain)

            这个演讲给了我很大的触动。很多内向者在融入社会的过程中强行让自己变外向合群，然而 Susan 在演讲中提到内向外向只是基因造成的。内向并不是坏事，内向者拥有众多外向者可能不具备的潜质，而很多成功者成功，恰恰是发挥了内向优势。Susan 站出来大声说内向者们并不孤独，一定会给很多人鼓励和力量。

            The power of introverts
            In a culture where being social and outgoing are prized above all else, it can be difficult, even shameful, to be an introvert. But, as Susan Cain argues in this passionate talk, introverts bring extraordinary talents and abilities to the world, and should be encouraged and celebrated.
              ted.com

            • Why we have too few women leaders (by Sheryl Sandberg)

              Sandberg 大名鼎鼎，她的 Lean In 一书也是十分出名。在演讲中她提到三点：

              1. 加入谈论：不要低估自己的能力，相信自己；

              2. 找个真正的人生伴侣：家庭和职场都很重要；

              3. 不要过早离开：不要过早退缩，多多寻求新机遇。

              Why we have too few women leaders
              Facebook COO Sheryl Sandberg looks at why a smaller percentage of women than men reach the top of their professions -- and offers 3 powerful pieces of advice to women aiming for the C-suite.
                ted.com

              • Really achieving your childhood dreams (by Randy Pausch)

                Randy Pausch 著名的最后一课非常值得一看，虽然这并不完全意义上属于 TED 演讲，不过 TED 官网上是有的。

                做演讲的时候，Pausch 离癌症宣判的死期仅有几个月，但看起来十分豁达乐观。他说：「面前的高墙并不是为了阻止我们，而是为了让我们有机会去证明自己有多想达到这个目标，这道墙是为了阻止那些不够渴望的人，它们是为了阻挡那些不够热爱的人而存在的。」这带给我很多触动。

                Really achieving your childhood dreams
                In 2007, Carnegie Mellon professor Randy Pausch, who was dying of pancreatic cancer, delivered a one-of-a-kind last lecture that made the world stop and pay attention. This moving talk will teach you how to really achieve your childhood dreams. Unmissable.
                  ted.com
